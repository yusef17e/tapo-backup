=====================================================
   TAPO CAMERA BACKUP — COMPLETE SETUP GUIDE
=====================================================

WHAT THIS DOES
--------------
This software automatically downloads your security camera
recordings from Tapo Cloud and saves them to your Google
Drive every night. It only saves footage from these times:

   Monday to Friday : 7:00 PM to 8:40 PM
   Sunday           : 11:00 AM to 1:00 PM

Old footage is automatically removed from Drive once it
reaches 12 GB so you never run out of storage.


WHAT YOU NEED BEFORE STARTING
------------------------------
  - A Windows computer
  - Your TP-Link / Tapo account email and password
  - A Google account (with Google Drive)
  - Internet access
  - About 20 minutes


=====================================================
   STEP 1 — COPY THIS FOLDER TO YOUR DESKTOP
=====================================================

Copy the entire TapoBackup folder to your Desktop.

IMPORTANT: Keep everything inside the folder together.
Do not rename the folder or move files around inside it.
The numbered .bat files are your setup tools — use them
in order.


=====================================================
   STEP 2 — INSTALL PYTHON
=====================================================

Python is the free programming language this software
runs on. You only need to install it once.

  1. Open a web browser and go to:
        https://www.python.org/downloads/

  2. Click the big yellow "Download Python" button.
     (Any version 3.8 or newer works — the latest
     version shown is always the right choice.)

  3. Open the file that downloads (it will look like
     "python-3.x.x-amd64.exe").

  4. IMPORTANT: At the bottom of the installer window,
     check the box that says:
        "Add Python to PATH"
     (This box is easy to miss — look for it!)

  5. Click "Install Now".

  6. When it says "Setup was successful", click Close.


=====================================================
   STEP 3 — INSTALL REQUIRED PACKAGES
=====================================================

Double-click:  1_Install_Packages.bat

A black window will open and show text scrolling.
Wait for it to say "Done! All packages installed."
Then close the window.

If you see a red error, try:
  Right-click the file → "Run as administrator"


=====================================================
   STEP 4 — SET UP GOOGLE DRIVE
=====================================================

This step has two parts: creating a folder in Drive,
and creating a "key" that lets the software upload to it.


--- PART A: Create a backup folder in Google Drive ---

  1. Go to drive.google.com in your browser.

  2. Click "+ New" on the left side.

  3. Click "Folder".

  4. Name it: Tapo Backup
     Click Create.

  5. Double-click the new "Tapo Backup" folder to open it.

  6. Look at the address bar at the top of your browser.
     It will show something like:
        https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1ABCxyz789

  7. The part after "/folders/" is your Folder ID.
     Example: 1ABCxyz789

     WRITE THIS DOWN — you will need it in Step 5.


--- PART B: Create a Google Cloud access key ---

This gives the software permission to upload to Drive.

  1. Go to: console.cloud.google.com
     Sign in with your Google account.

  2. At the very top of the page, click "Select a project"
     then click "NEW PROJECT".
     Name it: tapo-backup
     Click Create.
     Wait a few seconds for it to finish.

  3. In the search bar at the top, type:
        Google Drive API
     Click the result that says "Google Drive API".
     Click the blue "Enable" button.

  4. On the left menu, click:
        APIs & Services
     Then click: OAuth consent screen

     - Choose "External" and click Create.
     - App name: Tapo Backup
     - User support email: (your Google email)
     - Developer contact email: (your Google email)
     - Click "Save and Continue".
     - Click "Save and Continue" on the next two screens too.
     - On the screen that says "Test users":
         Click "+ Add Users"
         Type your Google email address
         Click Add, then Save
     - Click "Back to Dashboard".

  5. On the left menu, click:
        APIs & Services
     Then click: Credentials

     - Click "+ CREATE CREDENTIALS" near the top.
     - Click "OAuth client ID".
     - Application type: select "Desktop app"
     - Name: tapo-backup-desktop
     - Click Create.

  6. A pop-up appears. Click "DOWNLOAD JSON".
     A file will download to your Downloads folder.
     It will have a long name like:
        client_secret_12345-abcdef.apps.googleusercontent.com.json

  7. Rename that file to exactly:
        credentials.json
     (Remove everything else from the name.)

  8. Move that file into this folder:
        TapoBackup\tapo-drive-backup\credentials\
     (There is already a text file in there telling
     you this is the right place.)


=====================================================
   STEP 5 — EDIT THE CONFIG FILE
=====================================================

  1. Open this file in Notepad:
        TapoBackup\tapo-drive-backup\config.yaml

     To open it: right-click the file,
     choose "Open with", then choose "Notepad".

  2. Find this line near the top:
        gdrive_folder_id: "YOUR_FOLDER_ID_HERE"

  3. Replace YOUR_FOLDER_ID_HERE with the Folder ID
     you wrote down in Step 4a. Keep the quote marks.

     Example (yours will be different):
        gdrive_folder_id: "1ABCxyz789"

  4. Save the file: press Ctrl and S at the same time.
     Close Notepad.


=====================================================
   STEP 6 — LOG INTO TAPO
=====================================================

Double-click:  2_Tapo_Login.bat

A black window will open.
  - Type your TP-Link / Tapo email and press Enter.
  - Type your password and press Enter.
  - If it asks for a verification code, open your
    Tapo mobile app, go to Account settings, and
    find the 6-digit code. Type it and press Enter.

After logging in, the window will show a list of
your recent camera clips. That means it worked!

If you see an error, double-check your email and
password and run the file again.


=====================================================
   STEP 7 — CONNECT GOOGLE DRIVE
=====================================================

Double-click:  3_Google_Auth.bat

A black window will open, then your browser will open
with a Google sign-in page.

  1. Sign in with the same Google account you used
     in Step 4.

  2. You may see a screen that says:
        "Google hasn't verified this app"
     This is normal. Click "Advanced" (small text),
     then click "Go to Tapo Backup (unsafe)".

  3. Click "Allow" to give the software Drive access.

  4. The browser page will say:
        "The authentication flow has completed."
     You can close that browser tab.

  5. Back in the black window, you will see:
        "token.json saved to credentials/token.json"
     That means Google Drive is connected!

Close the black window when done.


=====================================================
   STEP 8 — RUN A TEST
=====================================================

Double-click:  4_Run_Backup.bat

The backup will run. You will see text appearing in
the black window. Wait for it to finish — it may
take a few minutes depending on how many clips
there are.

When it says "Backup finished", go to:
    drive.google.com

Open your "Tapo Backup" folder. You should see your
camera clips uploaded there.

If the folder is empty, it may be because there were
no clips recorded during the scheduled time windows
in the last 2 days. Try again tomorrow after 9 PM,
or temporarily set lookback_days to 7 in config.yaml
to pull in a week of footage.


=====================================================
   STEP 9 — SCHEDULE AUTOMATIC DAILY BACKUPS
=====================================================

Right-click:  5_Schedule_Daily.bat
Choose:       "Run as administrator"
Click Yes if Windows asks for permission.

You should see:
    "SUCCESS! The backup is now scheduled to run
     automatically every day at 9:00 PM."

IMPORTANT: After this step, do NOT move the TapoBackup
folder to a different location. The scheduled task
remembers where the folder is. If you need to move it,
run this file again from the new location.


=====================================================
   THAT'S IT — YOU'RE ALL SET!
=====================================================

The backup will now run automatically every night
at 9:00 PM. You do not need to do anything else.

Your camera clips will appear in the "Tapo Backup"
folder on Google Drive, named like:
    Living Room 2024-01-14 19-30-00.mp4
    Front Door 2024-01-14 19-30-00.mp4

Once Drive reaches 12 GB, the oldest clips are
automatically removed to make room for new ones.


=====================================================
   COMMON PROBLEMS AND FIXES
=====================================================

PROBLEM: "python is not recognized as a command"
FIX: Python was installed without "Add to PATH".
     Uninstall Python and install it again, making
     sure to check the "Add Python to PATH" box.
     Any version of Python 3.8 or newer will work.
     If unsure, just download the latest from
     python.org — it will always be compatible.

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

PROBLEM: "credentials.json not found"
FIX: You need to complete Step 4b and place the
     credentials.json file in the credentials folder.
     See Step 4b for details.

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

PROBLEM: "Set gdrive_folder_id in config.yaml"
FIX: You need to complete Step 5 and replace
     YOUR_FOLDER_ID_HERE with your real folder ID.

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

PROBLEM: Google auth window says "Access blocked"
FIX: You did not add yourself as a test user in
     Step 4b. Go back to console.cloud.google.com,
     APIs & Services → OAuth consent screen →
     Test users → Add your email. Then run
     3_Google_Auth.bat again.

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

PROBLEM: Backup runs but no clips appear in Drive
FIX: Check that your Tapo camera actually has
     cloud recording enabled in the Tapo mobile app.
     Open the app, tap your camera, and look for
     a "Cloud" or "Tapo Care" section. Also check
     that clips exist in the Tapo app for the
     scheduled time windows (Mon-Fri 7-8:40 PM,
     Sunday 11 AM-1 PM).

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

PROBLEM: Backup stopped working after weeks/months
FIX: The Google token may have expired.
     Double-click 3_Google_Auth.bat and sign in
     to Google again. This resets the connection.

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

PROBLEM: Tapo login stopped working
FIX: Double-click 2_Tapo_Login.bat and log in again.
     This sometimes happens after a password change
     or a long period without use.


=====================================================
   WHERE THINGS ARE STORED
=====================================================

Camera clips (temporary, deleted after upload):
   TapoBackup\tapo-drive-backup\downloads\

Backup log (what happened on each run):
   TapoBackup\tapo-drive-backup\logs\backup.log

Google credentials (do not delete):
   TapoBackup\tapo-drive-backup\credentials\

Settings file:
   TapoBackup\tapo-drive-backup\config.yaml
